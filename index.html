Would the world be at peace if humans understood each other?

Esperanto is a rich and nuanced language striving for simplicity and mutual understanding.

Esperanto was born in Poland in 1887.

Actually, Poland didn't exist as a country at the time. The territory that we now call Poland was divided among great powers.

Esperanto has a few thousands native speakers.

That's right: some children speak Esperanto with their parents. Granted, it's rarely their only native rongue: they usually speak Esperanto with one parent, another language with the other. But it's still quite an achievement for a constructed language!

There is an official Academy of Esperanto.

This Academy sits regularly to decide which new words to create, resolve linguistic disputes, and so on.
